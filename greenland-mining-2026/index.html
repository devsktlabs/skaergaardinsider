Greenland's Resource Awakening

Greenland, the world's largest island, has been on the mining industry's radar for decades. But 2026 marks a turning point. Updated permitting frameworks, surging Western geopolitical interest, and a critical minerals supply crisis are converging to make Greenland one of the most compelling frontier mining jurisdictions globally.

The Numbers

Greenland hosts world-class deposits across multiple mineral classes:

  • **Palladium & Gold**: The Skaergaard Project (25.4M oz PdEq, $68B in-situ value) — the largest undeveloped Western PGM deposit
  • **Rare Earth Elements**: Kuannersuit (Kvanefjeld) and other REE occurrences
  • **Zinc & Lead**: The Citronen Fjord deposit
  • **Iron, Copper, Nickel**: Multiple exploration targets across the island

    • Why 2026 Is Different

    Permitting Reform

    Greenland's updated 2025 exploitation licensing framework is designed to attract responsible resource development. The framework balances environmental protection with economic opportunity, providing clearer pathways from exploration to production.

    Geopolitical Alignment

    The EU's Critical Raw Materials Act explicitly targets diversification away from Russian and Chinese mineral supply. Greenland, as part of the Kingdom of Denmark and NATO, represents a geopolitically safe source of critical minerals. France has signaled readiness for minority equity stakes in Greenland projects. The UK and Canada are similarly increasing engagement.

    US Strategic Interest

    Increased US attention on Greenland has driven broader investor awareness of the territory's mineral potential. While political dynamics shift, the underlying geological case remains compelling.

    Key Active Projects

    Skaergaard Project (GRML)

    The flagship. 80% held by Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML), with an option for the remaining 20%. 25.4M oz palladium equivalent + 23.5M oz gold equivalent. Currently in EIA baseline with WSP Denmark A/S.

    Other Active Players

    Several companies are advancing projects across Greenland, though none match Skaergaard's scale in the PGM space.

    The Investment Case

    For investors evaluating Greenland mining exposure, several factors stand out:

  • **Resource scale**: World-class deposits across multiple mineral types
  • **Jurisdiction quality**: Denmark/NATO alignment eliminates sanctions risk
  • **Permitting trajectory**: 2025 framework provides a clearer development pathway
  • **Supply demand dynamics**: Western economies actively seeking non-Russian mineral sources
  • **ESG alignment**: Greenland has high environmental and social governance standards

    • Risks to Consider

  • **Infrastructure**: Greenland's remote location means higher development costs
  • **Timeline**: EIA and permitting processes take years, not months
  • **Commodity prices**: Project economics depend on sustained PGM and gold prices
  • **Political**: Greenland's home rule government can shift policy priorities
  • **Environmental**: Arctic ecosystems are sensitive; development must meet high standards

    • Conclusion

    Greenland's mining sector is at an inflection point. The combination of world-class geology, improving regulatory frameworks, and strong Western demand for critical minerals creates a compelling long-term thesis. The Skaergaard Project, with its scale and jurisdictional advantages, represents the most significant near-term development opportunity in the territory.
