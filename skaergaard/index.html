Project Overview

The Skaergaard intrusion hosts one of the world's most significant undeveloped PGM and gold deposits. The layered mafic intrusion contains palladium, gold, platinum, and copper mineralization within a well-defined geological setting.

Located in East Greenland near Ittoqqortoormiit, the project benefits from proximity to existing infrastructure from previous exploration campaigns. The resource is NI 43-101 compliant with 25.4M oz PdEq and 23.5M oz AuEq, exceeding $68B in gross in-situ value.

At current metal prices, this represents the largest undeveloped Western PGM deposit by a significant margin, positioning Greenland Mines as a critical supplier in the post-Russia-sanctions supply chain landscape.

Key Advantages

Scale

Largest undeveloped Western PGM resource. Ranks ahead of Wafi-Golpu, Golden Summit, and Snowfield.

Jurisdiction

Greenland's 2025 exploitation framework provides clear permitting pathways. Pro-mining government with EU backing.

Infrastructure

Existing camp infrastructure from previous Kvanefjeld work. Deepwater fjord access for port facilities.

Strategic Timing

Western supply chain restructuring creates urgent demand for non-Russian PGM supply sources.
