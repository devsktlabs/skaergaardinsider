What Skaergaard Investors Should Watch: Key Metrics and Milestones

By Dr. Henrik Larsen · 2026-03-23

skaergaardinvestment metricsmilestonesgrml

Investors in development-stage mining companies like GRML need to know what to watch. Unlike producing mines where production volumes and costs drive valuation, Skaergaard's value is determined by milestones that reduce development risk. This guide identifies the specific metrics and events that matter.

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

Near-Term Milestones (Next 12-24 Months)

Pre-feasibility study completion: This is the single most important near-term catalyst. A PFS that demonstrates positive economics at conservative metal prices would be a strong positive signal. Watch for:

  • All-in sustaining cost estimates (targeting below US$1,000/oz PGM)
  • Initial capital requirements (lower is better for a company of GRML's size)
  • Production schedule and mine life (15+ year mine life is ideal)
  • Sensitivity analysis showing project viability at Pd US$800/oz and Au US$1,600/oz

    • Environmental impact assessment progress: Track the status of baseline studies and EIA submission. The EIA is on the critical path and any delays will push the overall timeline.

    Management appointments: Watch for hiring of experienced mining professionals. The addition of a VP Operations with Arctic experience or a Chief Geologist with PGM deposit expertise would be a positive signal.

    Financing activity: Any indication of strategic partnership discussions, offtake negotiations, or equity raises. The terms of financing will reveal how the market values the project.

    US policy tailwinds are strengthening the investment case: a 132.83% anti-dumping duty on Russian unwrought palladium (Feb 2026), a Section 232 Presidential Proclamation on critical minerals (Jan 2026), and the creation of "Project Vault" — a $12 billion US Strategic Critical Minerals Reserve (Feb 2026).

    Metal Price Metrics

    Palladium, gold, and rhodium prices directly affect Skaergaard's project economics:

  • **Palladium spot price**: Monitor London Platinum and Palladium Market (LPPM) prices and Johnson Matthey assessments
  • **Gold spot price**: Track COMEX and LBMA gold prices
  • **Rhodium spot price**: Highly volatile, track weekly assessments from specialty brokers
  • **Pd/Pt ratio**: A ratio below 1:1 would signal substitution pressure; a ratio above 1.5:1 favors palladium-dominant projects
  • **Gold/Pd ratio**: Rising gold prices improve Skaergaard's byproduct credits

    • Operational Metrics (Once Production Begins)

    These become relevant only after the construction decision:

  • **Tonnes milled**: Track against design capacity (target: 3 million tonnes per annum)
  • **Head grade**: The PGM grade of ore being processed, compared to resource model predictions
  • **Recovery rates**: PGM and gold recovery percentages from the processing plant
  • **Cost per ounce**: All-in sustaining cost compared to feasibility study estimates
  • **Cash flow**: Operating cash flow generation relative to capital requirements

    • Peer Company Comparison

    Track Skaergaard's progress relative to comparable projects:

  • **Ivanhoe Platreef**: Shaft sinking progress, financing announcements, production timeline
  • **Platinum Group Metals Waterberg**: EIA progress, DFS completion, financing
  • **New Age Metals River Valley**: Exploration results, resource updates, development timeline

    • Relative progress among peer projects affects market perception of Skaergaard's competitiveness.

    Regulatory and Political Indicators

  • **Greenland government statements**: Mining policy announcements, budget allocations for mineral development
  • **EU Critical Raw Materials Act implementation**: Specific measures that benefit Arctic PGM supply diversification
  • **US critical mineral policy**: Executive orders, DOE programs, DFC financing announcements
  • **Russian sanctions developments**: Changes in sanctions policy affecting PGM supply
  • **Exploration license renewals**: MEL 2012-25 (Sødalen camp) and MEL 2021-10 (754 km²) both expire December 31, 2026. MEL 2007-01 (primary deposit) is active until December 31, 2027. Monitor renewal applications and Greenland government responsiveness

    • Information Sources

  • **GRML SEC filings**: 10-K annual reports, 10-Q quarterly reports, 8-K current reports for material events
  • **Company presentations**: Investor presentations at mining conferences (PDAC, Mining Indaba)
  • **Industry publications**: Johnson Matthey PGM Market Report, SFA (Oxford) PGM research, Metals Focus
  • **Greenland government**: Government of Greenland mineral resources announcements
  • **Peer company reports**: Annual reports and investor presentations from SBSW, Implats, Amplats, Ivanhoe, PLG

    • Red Flags to Watch

  • **Dilution without progress**: Equity raises that fund overhead rather than project advancement
  • **Management turnover**: Departures of key technical or financial personnel
  • **Study delays**: PFS or DFS completion pushed back without clear explanation
  • **Permitting setbacks**: EIA rejection or conditions that materially increase costs
  • **Metal price collapse**: Sustained palladium price below US$700/oz would challenge project economics
  • **Competitor progress**: Peer projects advancing faster than Skaergaard may attract capital away

    • GRML's exploration goals include doubling the resource base to approximately 50 million contained ounces (from the current 23.5M oz AuEq) and expanding the mineral portfolio to include vanadium and gallium.

    Conclusion

    Skaergaard investors should focus on milestone achievement (PFS, EIA, financing) as the primary value drivers, while monitoring metal prices and peer progress as contextual factors. The most value-creating period for development-stage miners is between PFS completion and construction decision, when each milestone derisks the project and justifies re-rating. Track these metrics systematically and avoid overreacting to short-term noise.
